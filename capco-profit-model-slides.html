How Capco Makes Profit — and Where the Model Breaks Down

01

Opportunity Identification

Client need surfaces via RFP or proactive pitch. Assess: What does the client need? What is their budget, authority, and timeline?

02

Shaping & Pricing

Build the team pyramid (AO→Partner). Plug resources into the global pricing model: grade, location, bill rate, duration, holidays, PTO. Choose T&M or Fixed Price.

03

Revenue Generation

Contract signed with TCV. Fixed Price = even monthly payments. T&M = billable days × rate per month. Revenue recognized as team delivers.

04

Profit = The Arbitrage

Profit margin = gap between client bill rate and Capco's standard cost (salary + benefits). Target: 50%+ margin. Lower grades = higher margin.

⚠ Revenue Leakage

  • Approved budget left un-billed because team worked fewer days than planned (sick days, excess PTO)
  • Client refuses milestone payments due to deliverable disputes
  • T&M engagements under-tracked on timesheets

⚠ Scope & Effort Overruns

  • Delivery takes more hours than priced — bugs, complexity, rework consume the buffer
  • Fixed price contracts absorb the extra cost, eroding margin
  • Insufficient buffer / contingency in original pricing model

📈 Market Pressure

  • Competitors willing to price lower — forces Capco to drop bill rates
  • Clients paying less for resource augmentation work
  • Margins compressed over last 2–5 years industry-wide

📈 Utilization & Bench Cost

  • Senior resources (PC/MP/ED) split across projects at part-time allocation to stay off bench
  • Investment-rate staffing to protect relationships — Capco absorbs cost for strategic value
  • Bench time = cost with zero revenue offset
💡

My View as an SC Leading an AI Program at Jefferies

I operate above my level daily — leading a 26-person engineering team, owning the JefAI roadmap, and presenting to C-level stakeholders. The pricing model breaks down here: Capco bills me at SC rate while the work I deliver is PC/MP-level scope and responsibility. This is both a promotion signal and a commercial opportunity — billing at the appropriate grade would better reflect the value delivered and improve revenue per engagement, turning my impact into margin upside for Capco.
